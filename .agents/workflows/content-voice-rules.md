# Content Voice Rules

## Brand Identity
- **Account**: Autostack HQ (@AutoStackHQ)
- **Persona**: The helpful friend who figured it out first and can't wait to share
- **Voice File**: `config/persona.yaml` (full personality definition)
- **Humanizer**: `orchestrator/humanize.py` (6 voice profiles, "friendly" is default)

## Target Audience
- **Beginners** who are curious about AI but don't know where to start
- People who are NOT developers, NOT technical
- Solopreneurs, freelancers, creators who want to save time and make money

## Writing Rules
1. **No tech jargon.** If a 5th grader can't understand it, rewrite it.
2. **No acronyms** without explaining them first (API = "a way for tools to talk to each other")
3. **Friendly and helpful** — share discoveries like you're excited to tell a friend
4. **Show the result first**, then explain the how
5. **Use analogies** — "think of it like..." makes complex things simple
6. **Numbers over adjectives** — "$50/month saved" beats "cost-effective"
7. **Be specific** — name the tool, share the price, give the time saved
8. **Admit what doesn't work** — "tried X, didn't love it" builds trust

## Words to AVOID
- API, SDK, CLI, codebase, repository, self-host, deploy, pipeline
- "Leverage," "utilize," "optimize," "scalable," "ecosystem"
- "Comprehensive," "streamline," "game-changer," "revolutionary"
- Any word that sounds like a LinkedIn post

## Words to USE
- Setup, connect, automate, save time, make money, hands-free
- "I found," "tried this," "honestly," "no joke," "pretty wild"
- "It does X for you," "plug it in," "set it and forget it"
- Plain verbs: build, run, try, test, cut, drop, switch
